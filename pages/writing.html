The Future of Moore's, Kryder's, and Robert's Laws

Understanding Moore's, Kryder's, and Robert's Laws helps establish links between relevant signals in technology topics that appear unrelated–such as 5G Wireless Technology, the Internet of Things, and the surge of interest collecting retro games and comics–to help in forecasting possible futures. This article will explore examples of how these laws can predict future signals and inform forecasts for future technology.

The EU’s General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

When the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) launched in May of 2018, the European Union (EU) introduced new information security challenges. Critics suggest the GDPR poses a threat to advancements in emerging technologies such as Artificial Intelligence. With the EU being the first entity to implement formal legal controls, the rest of the world will have an opportunity to learn from the successes and failures of the GDPR.

The Loss of the Florida Everglades

The Florida Everglades are declining due to global climate change and human impact, which result in degraded soil and water quality as well as reduced freshwater reserves. This presentation leverages a systems analysis approach to identify the environmental impacts caused by these pressures.

Publication

Can Enterprise Architecture Save the Dinosaurs?

In this highly original and creative paper, Allison Adolphson uses the lens of Enterprise Architecture to look at the problems that beset the original Jurassic Park facility in the seminal 1993 movie of the same name. In understanding the circumstances that unfolded throughout that movie, Allison identifies key areas of change needed to ensure that future operations don’t fall prey (pun intended) to the same issues. This not only identifies areas of technological challenge, but business operations, strategic alignment and more.
